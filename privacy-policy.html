Privacy Policy - Study Together

Last Updated: January 8, 2026

1. Introduction

Study Together ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

  • Account Information: Email address and username (either provided directly or from your Google account)
  • Study Session Data: Session duration, start and end times, room codes, and participant information
  • Usage Statistics: Total study hours, number of completed sessions, and focus violations
  • Authentication Data: Login credentials and authentication tokens

3. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used to:

  • Create and maintain your user account
  • Facilitate study sessions with other users
  • Track your study progress and statistics
  • Enable accountability features (automatic violation detection)
  • Provide session reports and performance analytics
  • Improve our service and user experience

4. Data Storage and Security

All data is stored securely using Google Firebase services:

  • Firebase Authentication: Manages user accounts and login sessions
  • Cloud Firestore: Stores user profiles and session data
  • Data is encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Firebase complies with SOC 2, ISO 27001, and other security standards

5. Data Sharing and Visibility

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Your data visibility:

  • Private: Your email address is never shared with other users
  • Session Participants: Your username and session activity are visible to participants in your study sessions
  • No Third Parties: We do not share your data with advertisers or data brokers

6. Google Sign-In

When you sign in with Google:

  • We receive your email address and display name from Google
  • This information is used solely for authentication and account creation
  • We follow Google's OAuth 2.0 policies and API Services User Data Policy
  • Google's use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements

7. Automatic Violation Detection

Our app uses React Native's AppState API to detect when you leave the app during study sessions:

  • We only monitor app state during active study sessions
  • We do not track which apps you switch to
  • We do not access your device's app usage data outside of our app
  • A 5-second grace period is applied before logging a violation

8. Your Rights and Control

You have the right to:

  • Access: View all your personal data in the Account History screen
  • Delete: Use the "Wipe History" feature to permanently delete all your sessions and reset statistics
  • Update: Modify your account information at any time
  • Export: Request a copy of your data by contacting us
  • Account Deletion: Permanently delete your account and all associated data

9. Data Retention

We retain your data as follows:

  • Active Accounts: Data is kept while your account is active
  • Session History: Stored until you delete it using "Wipe History"
  • Deleted Accounts: All data is permanently deleted within 30 days of account deletion

10. Children's Privacy

Study Together is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to delete such information.

11. International Users

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that are different from the laws of your country. By using Study Together, you consent to such transfers.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by:

  • Updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy
  • Posting a notice in the app (for material changes)
  • Sending an email notification (for significant changes)

Your continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

13. Third-Party Services

Our app uses the following third-party services:

  • Google Firebase: Authentication and database services
  • Google Sign-In: OAuth authentication
  • Expo: App development and deployment platform

These services have their own privacy policies governing how they handle your data.

14. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to know if personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Right to opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell data)
  • Right to deletion
  • Right to non-discrimination

15. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right of access
  • Right to rectification
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object
  • Right to withdraw consent
